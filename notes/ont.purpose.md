## Why The Semantic Flow Ontology Exists

The ontology exists to support the creation and maintenance of Semantic Flow identifiers that are human-dereferenceable and machine-usable in a predictable way. It formalizes how a Semantic Flow designator, its associated `Knop`, and the `Knop`'s supporting artifacts and optional payload resource fit together, so tools can consistently discover what an identifier is about and how its supporting Semantic Flow artifacts are organized.

It also exists to formalize the core structure of digital artifacts: their continuity over time, their historical states, their manifestations, and the located files that provide retrievable bytes. That structure makes it possible to model publishing and editing workflows cleanly, including immutable access to historical versions, without tying the model to any one serialization strategy or requiring every nearby file to be part of a mesh.
